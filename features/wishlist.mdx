The Book Paradise wishlist gives you a frictionless way to bookmark titles you are interested in without committing to a purchase right away. Whether you are building a reading list, saving gift ideas, or waiting for the right moment to buy, the wishlist lets you flag any of the 24 catalog titles with a single click. Like the cart, the wishlist is backed entirely by your browser's `localStorage`, so your saved books are always there when you return — no account or login required.

## Adding to Your Wishlist

Every book card in the catalog displays a **heart icon** (♡) in its top-right corner.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find a book you want to save">
    Browse or filter the Products section until you spot a title you would like to keep for later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the heart icon">
    Click the heart icon on the book card. This calls `toggleWishlist(bookId)`, which adds the book's ID to `bp_wishlist` in `localStorage`. The icon transitions from an outlined heart (♡) to a filled, colored heart (♥) instantly — no page reload required.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove from wishlist">
    Click the same filled heart icon again to call `toggleWishlist(bookId)` a second time. The icon returns to its outlined state and the book's ID is removed from `localStorage`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Viewing Your Wishlist

You can access your wishlist in two ways:

- **Inline highlighting** — books that are currently in your wishlist are visually distinguished in the main catalog grid with a highlighted heart icon, so you can see your saved titles at a glance while browsing.
- **Wishlist sidebar** — click the **View Wishlist** button in the hero section or the wishlist icon in the navbar to open a dedicated panel that lists only your wishlisted books, making it easy to review everything you have saved in one place.

## Moving a Book to the Cart

You do not need to navigate back to the main catalog to purchase a wishlisted title. Each book in the wishlist view has its own **Add to Cart** button. Clicking it adds the book to your cart (see [Shopping Cart](/features/shopping-cart) for details) without removing it from the wishlist — you decide when, or whether, to remove the heart.

## Persistence

Your wishlist is stored in `localStorage` as a compact array of book IDs. This keeps the stored data minimal while still allowing the application to reconstruct the full wishlist by cross-referencing the `books` array in `script.js`.

```js Wishlist data shape
// localStorage key: "bp_wishlist"
[1, 7, 12]  // Array of book IDs
```

The wishlist is read from `localStorage` on every page load and used to restore the filled-heart state on all matching book cards, so your wishlist always looks exactly as you left it.

<Note>
  Wishlist data is stored only in your current browser on your current device. It is not synced across different browsers, other devices, or private/incognito windows. If you clear your browser's site data, your wishlist will be reset.
</Note>
